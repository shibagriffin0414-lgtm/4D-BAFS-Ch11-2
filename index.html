Non-current assets provide long-term benefits (>1 accounting period). Under the accrual accounting concept, the cost is not fully expensed in the year of acquisition. Instead, it is capitalised and allocated via depreciation.

Definition: Depreciation is the systematic allocation of the cost of a tangible non-current asset over its useful life.

Underlying Concept: Matching Concept

The cost of the asset must be allocated and matched against the revenues (benefits) it generates over the periods it is used.

Factors affecting useful life:

  • Physical deterioration (wear and tear, rust, decay).
  • Depletion (e.g., coal mines, oil wells).
  • Economic factors (obsolescence due to tech/fashion, or inadequacy).

Capital Expenditure

  • Long-term benefits (> 1 year).
  • Includes purchase price + directly attributable costs (freight, installation, testing) to bring asset to location/condition for use.
  • Includes extension/improvement to existing assets.
  • Double Entry: Dr Non-current asset | Cr Cash/Bank

Revenue Expenditure

  • Short-term benefits (day-to-day operations).
  • Wholly expensed in the period incurred.
  • Examples: Rent, wages, petrol, regular insurance, repairs.
  • Double Entry: Dr Expense | Cr Cash/Bank
Answering Tip - Repairs: Normally revenue expenditure. Exception: Repairs made to a second-hand asset *before* it is ready for its intended use are treated as capital expenditure (e.g., fixing the engine on a newly purchased used van).
{/* Straight Line */}

1. Straight-Line Method

Charges a constant amount each period, assuming even benefits over time.

Depreciation =
Cost - Residual Value Useful Life
{/* Reducing Balance */}

2. Reducing-Balance Method

Charge diminishes each year. Useful for assets that generate more benefits early in their life (or have rising repair costs later).

Depreciation = Net Book Value (NBV) × Fixed % Rate
{/* Usage Based */}

3. Usage-Based Method

Charge varies with actual usage (hours or output).

Depreciation = (Cost - Residual) ×
Actual output this period Total estimated output

Annual Depreciation Charge

Disposal of Non-Current Assets

Follow these 4 steps in the Disposal Account:

  1. // Remove original cost
    Dr Disposal
    Cr Non-current asset
  2. // Remove accumulated depreciation

    Cr Disposal
